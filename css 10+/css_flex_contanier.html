The flex container Properties

  • flex-direction
  • flex-wrap
  • align-items
  • justify-content
  • align-content

flex-direction

  • row: Default value. Items are placed horizontally, from left to right.
  • row-reverse: Items are placed horizontally, from right to left
  • column: Items are placed vertically, from top to bottom
  • column-reverse: Items are placed vertically, from bottom to top

flex-wrap

  • nowrap: (Default) Flex items are placed on a single line.
  • wrap: Flex items will wrap onto multiple lines if there's not enough space for them on a single line
  • wrap-reverse: Similar to wrap, but the order of the lines is reversed

    • Difference between flex-wrap & white-space

  • flex-wrap This property controls whether the flex items within a flex container are forced onto one line or can wrap onto multiple lines
  • white-space controls how white space inside an element is handled, including whether text should wrap to the next line or not.

align-items

(align-items usually controls vertical alignment)

  • stretch (default): Items stretch to fill the container's height
  • flex-start: Items align at the top.
  • flex-end: Items align at the bottom
  • center: Items align in the center vertically
  • baseline: Items align based on their text baselines

    • Application

  • Mobile-Responsive Design
  • Equal Height Columns
  • Vertical Centering of Elements

justify-content

The justify-content align items horizontally or vertically depending on the direction of the main axies(flex-direction)

  • flex-start: Items align to the start
  • flex-end: Items align to the end
  • center: Items center along the main axis
  • space-between: Even spacing between items, with the first item at the start and the last at the end
  • space-around: Even spacing around items, with half-space on the edges.

    • Application

  • Navigation Bars: space-between spreads links evenly
  • Button Groups: space-around provides uniform spacing
  • Footers: space-evenly distributes elements like icons
  • Hero Sections: center aligns content in the middle

align-content

align-content is used to arrange items in multiple rows or columns within a container when there is extra space. It determines how those rows or coloumns are positioned and spaced within the container.
